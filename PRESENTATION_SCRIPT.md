## Script

### 1 · Title  — *0:00*
> [Slide 1: "Clavis — The AI compliance analyst"]

"Compliance is a two-hundred-billion-dollar industry — and most of it still
runs on one person, copy-pasting a name into a database. I'm [NAME], and this
is **Clavis: the AI compliance analyst.** In five minutes: the problem, and a
product that does the whole screening in seconds."

### 2 · The problem — *0:18*
> [Slide 2 — stats: ~3 hrs analyst time · $2,000+ · $200B+/yr]

"Here's the reality. A single corporate KYC review eats **about three hours** of
a skilled analyst's time — and costs **over two thousand dollars.** Multiply
that across the industry and it's a **two-hundred-billion-dollar** annual spend.
It's slow, it's expensive, and today it only scales by hiring more people."

### 3 · Why it breaks — *0:48*
> [Slide 3 — the human-error list]

"And the weakest link is the human in the loop. Data-entry typos. Misread
documents. Confirmation bias. Two analysts looking at the same person and
reaching **different verdicts.** Every one of these is a crack in the wall."

### 4 · The chain reaction — *1:00*
> [Slide 4 — Human error → False match → Alert fires → Hours lost]

"And one small mistake doesn't stay small. A single typo creates a false match.
The match fires an alert. The alert burns hours of investigation. **Ninety to
ninety-five percent** of every alert a team investigates is a false positive —
pure noise. Analysts spend most of their day clearing it, instead of catching
real risk."

### 5 · The stakes — *1:30*
> [Slide 5 — ~$4B fines · $1B+ crypto · 90% of banks]

"And getting it wrong is no longer survivable. In 2025 alone — roughly **four
billion dollars** in AML and KYC fines, over a billion on crypto alone. Ninety
percent of banks say human error directly hits their risk decisions.
Regulatory exposure, reputational damage, the wrong counterparty — pick your
nightmare."

### 6 · The solution — *1:54*
> [Slide 6 — Enter → Agent screens → Audit-ready report]

"This is Clavis. You enter a subject — a name, a country. An AI agent runs the
**entire** screening: two-hundred-plus watchlists, **one-point-four million**
sanctions entries, live adverse-media search across the web. And it hands you
an **audit-ready report** — cited evidence trail, downloadable PDF."

### · Live demo — *2:08*  *(≈1:10, OFF-DECK — no slide)*
> [No slide. Switch to the browser: demo video, then `/screen?mock=1`.]

**Setup (spoken):**
"Let me show you. First, a real screening — start to finish."

**[PLAY VIDEO — ~20s. Stay silent; let it run.]**

"That's the real product, running live."

**[LIVE DEMO — switch to `/screen?mock=1` for a reliable, fast run.]**
"Let me run one myself. High-risk subject: Viktor Yanukovych, a former head of
state."
- [Click the **"Yanukovych — HIGH RISK"** demo chip → form fills.]
- [Click **Run screening.**]

"Watch the agent work — sanctions and PEP, then adverse media…" *(let phases
stream ~10–15s)* "…and there's the verdict: **high risk**, scored, with the

seconds."

### 7 · Measurable outcome — *3:18*
> [Slide 7 — worked example: 10 FoF · 5 yrs · 220 investors → €21K vs ~€3K]

"And the outcome is measurable in euros. Take ten fund-of-funds over five years —
two hundred and twenty investors to screen and monitor. Done manually, that's
roughly six hundred reviews, an hour each, at thirty-plus euros an hour —
**twenty-one thousand euros.** With Clavis, one to ten minutes per report,
**about three thousand** — a **seven-times** cost reduction. And the same
analysts now screen far more, or you simply run a leaner team."

### 8 · Why it matters — *3:36*
> [Slide 8 — four pillars]

"So Clavis pays for itself four ways: **faster and more reliable**, **no human
bias**, **major cost savings**, and **legal and reputational protection.**"

### 9 · Market — *3:50*
> [Slide 9 — TAM $200B+ · SAM ~$11B · SOM ~$300M]

"And the market is mandatory. Financial-crime compliance is a **two-hundred-
billion-dollar** annual spend — that's our TAM. The KYC and AML software we
displace is **eleven billion** by 2030. Our beachhead — funds, crypto and
mid-market fintech in the EU — is **three hundred million.** And here's the
thing: **every fund in this room is a buyer** — you screen your LPs, your GPs,
your deal counterparties. This is a tool you already need."

### 10 · Competitor analysis — *4:18*
> [Slide 10 — 2×2: x = data→decision, y = manual→autonomous; Clavis top-right]

"So why hasn't this been solved? Everyone sells a **piece.** Down here,
World-Check and LexisNexis sell the **lists** — analyst-driven, you chase every
hit. ComplyAdvantage and Sumsub raise the **flags.** Newer AI tools like
Greenlite automate one step. **Clavis sits alone in the top-right** — fully
autonomous, and the only one that hands back a finished, cited decision."

### 11 · Business model — *4:44*
> [Slide 11 — Pilot €3K/mo · Platform from €30K/yr · Enterprise]

"We sell **enterprise-first.** A paid **pilot** at three thousand a month proves
value. That converts to a **platform** license from thirty thousand a year —
seats, monitoring, SSO. And **enterprise** for high volume. Target contract value
north of thirty thousand — every customer is a contract, not a credit card."

### 12 · Go-to-market — *5:10*
> [Slide 12 — Fund network → Integrations → Directories]

"And we already have the way in. We **land** through our **own network of funds**
— warm intros, first design partners, screening their LPs and counterparties
from day one, across our footprint in the Netherlands, the UK, France and
Bulgaria. We **integrate** with the data and platforms — Interpol notices,
OpenSanctions, the onboarding stacks. Then we **distribute** through RegTech
directories and partner deals. And your portfolio? That's our first customers."

### 13 · Why us — *5:38*
> [Slide 13 — Gekov · Manolov · Parpulov · Yordanoff]

"And we're the team to build it. **Alexander Gekov**, founder of TalentSight and
an engineer at n8n. **Vladislav Manolov**, data scientist at Nexo — he lived
this exact problem inside big tech. **Dimitar Parpulov**, software developer at
Tuk-Tam. And **Mario Yordanoff**, who runs his own agency and drives our growth.
We're close observers of the venture field, with international exposure across
four countries — and we've felt this pain first-hand."

### 14 · The ask — *6:06*
> [Slide 14 — €150K for 10% · 55/30/10/5]

"We're raising **a hundred and fifty thousand euros for ten percent** — about a
year of runway. **Most of it goes into product** — closing the gap with
incumbents on monitoring, data coverage and case management — then into
converting our warm fund pipeline into paying contracts, with a little for data,
SOC 2 and operations. The goal: **feature parity, five-plus paying fund
partners, and first recurring revenue — ready to raise a seed.**"

### 15 · Closing — *6:34*
> [Slide 15 — "Stop manual screening. Start deciding."]

"**Stop manual screening. Start deciding.** That's Clavis. Come run a live
screening with us — thank you."

## Appendix (slides 16–17) — Q&A backup, not presented
- **16 · Defensibility / moat** — answer to "what's your moat?": proprietary
  screening workflow across 200+ watchlists, every run improves the
  false-positive filter, audit-trail + monitoring data create switching costs,
  SOC 2 / ISO are a barrier newcomers can't skip.
- **17 · Trust & accuracy** — answer to "how do you handle AI hallucination /
  liability?": every claim is cited, sanctions/PEP matching is deterministic
  (the LLM only summarises), conservative low-confidence flags on ambiguous
  identities, optional human-in-the-loop.
